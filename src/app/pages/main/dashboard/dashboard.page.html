Welcome to GGIA tool!

The ESPON GGIA tool is designed to quantify the climate impact of spatial plans and policies in territories, cities and municipalities all over Europe. GGIA provides two alternative modes for greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions quantification: Territorial mode consists of sectoral modules and quantifies the direct GHG emissions caused within the boundaries of the area in concern. Consumption-based mode quantifies the GHG emissions caused by the consumption of the residents of the area, wherever these emission may occur. The results of consumption-based quantification cover all sectors of consumption and are directly comparable.​

The first step is to create a baseline – to estimate the absolute emissions of the area. ​
The second step is to quantify the impact of a plan or a policy .

​You can start calculation without any expert knowledge on GHG quantification. For more accurate results, the experts in Your organisation can create a local dataset and upload it in the GGIA tool. This way the quantification can be aligned with the previous GHG analyses of the region, and the most accurate source data is available. The local datasets are not validated but they are available for all users of the tool. GGIA enables comparison of best practises and policy impacts across the regions of Europe.​ As the quantification results are estimates that highly depend on the input values, the provider of GGIA, ESPON EGTC, cannot guarantee the authenticity of the results and cannot be held responsible for any decisions taken based on the results from the GGIA tool. ESPON GGIA source code is available in GitHub. ​
